Information Architecture: Skincare Website

This project was submtted as part of the Informaton Architecture module of the MSc Human-Computer Interaction Design.

Summary: I conducted interviews, competitor analysis and online card sorting exercise to gather Information Architecture (IA) requirements. I designed an IA for a Skincare Website for young adults suffering from acne.

My deliverables included: Domain Model, Site Map, User Journey, and Wireframes of the website.

Domain Model

To better understand my domain, I interviewed three domain experts and conducted a competitior analysis on four different skincare websites and blogs.

Site Map

I reviewed the terminology of several skincare websites which were mentioned during the interviews with skincare consumers and used the common terms and categories to curate an open card sorting exercise.

I conducted an open card sort exercise (on OptimalWorkshop) with five participants to clarify labels and navigation which helped me create my sitemap structure.


User Journey

I requested the users to locate a product for the task scenario because I felt this task would allow more exploration, use of global navigation and filtered search.

Wireframes

Every page on my website has a breadcrumb that helps with forwards and backwards navigation.


Wireframe 1 is an educational page that teaches the users what acne is, how to treat acne and the common causes of acne. From the interviews, these three topics were the most common information skincare consumers wanted to know.

I have opted for a Vertical Navigation on the left side as it increases findability for the user and supports efficient scanning of the website compared to horizontal navigation.

Wireframe 2 is a shopping section where users can shop products and filter products based on the product type, brand, ingredients, skin concerns, skin type, skin concerns, SPF content, price range and average reviews.

The most common way for users to find their skincare product was by brand, ingredients and skin concerns, so I have incorporated them in my wireframe.

Wireframe 3 is a product page. The page details the product's name, a short description of the product, price and quantity. During the interview with skincare consumers, most participants said that they always look at the reviews before deciding to buy a product.

From this, I made sure that reviews are easy to see and have different methods of sorting the reviews. The product page also recommends similar products and articles where the product is featured.

Wireframe 4 is an Online Forum where users can talk about anything to do with skincare.

During the interview with skincare consumers, users expressed that to keep up with skincare news and trends online forums e.g Reddit was mostly used to share information and thoughts with one another.
