The Kindness Box.

This project was submtted as part of the Interaction Design module of the MSc Human-Computer Interaction Design at City, University of London.

Summary: Research Team project where we created an interactive vending machine for kindness from ideation, user research, prototyping and evaluation.

Deliverables included: Written report, Personas, User Journeys, High-Fideliity prototype and a Poster.
Members involved: Erica Saffron (Me), Sriram Venkitachalam, Iriani Amirudin, Karen Leung
Access the Written Report here.
Access the High-Fidelity Prototype here.

Project Overview

We were tasked to design a creative and tailored interactive digital technology that will enhance the experience of visitors to an open-air space by enabling visitors to engage in an "act of kindness" in the space.

We chose King's Cross Station as our open-air space as it is a major transportation hub with travellers and commuters from all over the UK and Europe, and the space is unique as there's a diverse range of visitors at the station.

User Research

We interviewed and observed people at the King's Cross Station.

Personas

We created two personas based on our user research. Each persona represents a user who wants to either give or recieve a form of kindness.



Existing User Journeys

We created the current user journey based on our interviews conducted at the station. We incorporated quotes from our interviews in the user journeys and created one user journey each for our Personas.



Conceptual Design

We also set up a cardboard prototype which had the question:
"If there was a vending machine for kindness at King's Cross what would you give/take?" and a pen where people can write responses on the paper.

Future User Journeys

Every page on my website has a breadcrumb that helps with forwards and backwards navigation.




Detailed Design

Following Conceptual Design, we created a high-fidelity prototype and 3D Visualisation of the vending machine on-site.


Poster

Poster which details what The Kindness Box is about and how to use the machine, with a 3D visual representation of how the vending machine looks on-site at King's Cross Station.
